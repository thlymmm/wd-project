🌱 PLANT SPECIES

Alpinia purpurata (Red Ginger)

A perennial herbaceous plant with bright red spike inflorescences, widely used for cut flowers, landscape decoration, and garden beautification.

Pseuderanthemum ‘golden’ (Golden False Eranthemum)

An evergreen shrub featured for its bright golden-yellow foliage, serving as an excellent color-contrast plant in tropical gardens.

Piper sarmentosum (Wild Betel Leaf)

A creeping herb widely used in Southeast Asian cooking to wrap food, while also carrying well-known traditional medicinal values.

Dieffenbachia seguine

A popular tropical evergreen perennial from the Araceae family, highly prized for its large, dramatic leaves splattered with creamy-white or yellow variegation.

Fragraea ceilanica

These blossoms emit an incredibly sweet, rich perfume, making it a favorite ornamental plant for gardens and patios.

Calathea zebrina (Zebra Plant)

It is celebrated for its magnificent, velvety leaves of bright green, boldly patterned with zebra-like dark green stripes, and its characteristic "prayer plant" habit of folding its leaves at night.

Pisonia alba (Lettuce Tree）

It is highly valued in landscaping for its beautiful, thin, and wavy leaves that emerge in a brilliant shade of pale yellow or creamy-white before maturing into a soft chartreuse.

Spathoglottis Plicata(Large Purple Orchid)

Unlike epiphytic orchids that grow on trees, it roots firmly in the soil, producing elegant, pleated leaves that resemble palm seedlings and tall spikes of charming, rich purple to magenta blossoms that flower repeatedly throughout the year.

Clerodendrum paniculatum (Pagoda Flower）

It features massive, upright, terminal inflorescences up to 45 cm tall, packed with hundreds of fiery orange-red or scarlet tubular flowers arranged in tiers like a traditional Buddhist pagoda.

🌿 EUPHORBIACEAE FAMILY

Plants with milky sap, varied forms for ornamental or industrial uses (Click to back to top ↑)

Euphorbia pulcherrima (Poinsettia)

Commonly known as Poinsettia, Euphorbia pulcherrima is a popular tropical shrub famous for its vibrant red and green foliage widely used in Christmas floral displays.

Euphorbia hirta

A pantropical annual herb with hairy stems and small dense flower clusters, widely recognized for its traditional medicinal properties and ecological resilience.

Manihot esculenta (Cassava)

A woody shrub native to South America, Manihot esculenta features distinctive palmate leaves and is globally cultivated for its starchy tuberous roots.

Hippomane mancinella (Manchineel)

Famously known as the Manchineel tree, Hippomane mancinella is a coastal evergreen tree with apple-like fruits, widely recognized as one of the world's most toxic plants.

Jatropha curcas

A hardy tropical shrub with dark green trilobed leaves, widely recognized for its high drought resistance and oil-rich seeds used in biofuel production.

Acalypha wilkesiana

An eye-catching evergreen shrub featuring vibrant, multicolored coppery leaves, widely used as an ornamental hedge or accent plant in tropical landscapes.

Jatropha podagrica

An unusual tropical shrub characterized by its swollen, bottle-like stem and bright coral-red flower clusters that bloom throughout the year.

Codiaeum variegatum

A highly popular tropical evergreen shrub celebrated for its brilliantly colored, glossy leathery leaves that display endless patterns of yellow, red, and green.

Euphorbia milii (Crown of Thorns)

It is characterized by densely spiny stems and colorful, long-lasting flower bracts (usually red, pink, or yellow) surrounding the tiny true flowers.

🌿 POLYGONACEAE FAMILY

Commonly known as the knotweed family, featuring swollen nodes on stems (Click to back to top ↑)

Antigonon leptopus

A vigorous tropical climbing vine featuring cascading clusters of vibrant pink flowers and heart-shaped leaves, perfect for trellises, fences, and arbors.

Coccoloba uvifera

A hardy coastal tree or shrub featuring large, leathery circular leaves and clusters of grape-like fruits, widely used for beach stabilization and tropical landscaping.

Persicaria capitata

A charming prostrate perennial herb featuring distinct V-marked leaves and small, ball-like pink flower heads, widely used as an attractive ornamental groundcover.

Muehlenbeckia complexa

A unique, tangling deciduous vine featuring slender dark wire-like stems and tiny rounded leaves, widely used for topiary, groundcover, or cascading over garden walls.

Fagopyrum esculentum

An erect annual herb featuring distinctive heart-shaped leaves and dense clusters of small white flowers, widely cultivated as a highly nutritious pseudocereal.

Homalocladium platycladum

An extraordinary tropical shrub characterized by its flat, lime-green ribbon-like stems and jointed segments, widely grown as a striking architectural curiosity in gardens.

Rumex acetosa

A hardy perennial herb featuring large arrow-shaped leaves and tall spikes of reddish-green flowers, widely grown for both culinary and ornamental use.

Persicaria odorata

A perennial aromatic herb featuring dark green leaves with a distinct dark chevron.

Persicaria hydropiper (Water Pepper)

A moisture-loving annual herb known for its pungent, spicy leaves. It is widely used in traditional herbal medicine.

🌿 ACANTHACEAE FAMILY

An important tropical ornamental family with uniquely structured bracts (Click to back to top ↑)

Pseuderanthemum carruthersii (Purple False Eranthemum)

A tropical evergreen shrub prized for its striking variegated foliage of deep purple, pink, and bronze tones.

Aphelandra squarrosa (Zebra Plant)

A tropical evergreen shrub native to Brazil, highly prized for its dramatic dark green leaves striped with vivid ivory-white veins. It also produces spikes of brilliant yellow flower bracts that can last for several weeks.

Fittonia albivenis (Nerve Plant)

It is deeply loved for its lush green foliage woven with contrasting, mosaic-like veins of white, red, or pink, thriving exceptionally well in high-humidity terrariums.

Acanthus mollis (Bear's Breeches)

It features large, glossy, sculptural leaves and dramatic tall spikes of hooded white flowers nestled in purple bracts.

Justicia carnea (Flamingo Flower)

It is striking for its large, heavily-veined foliage and large, showy heads of curved, bright pink to crimson tubular flowers that resemble a cluster of plumes or feathers.

Thunbergia erecta (King's Mantle)

Unlike other vining species in its genus, it grows as a dense bush, producing a prolific display of velvety dark-blue or purple trumpet flowers highlighted by a contrasting yellow center.

Andrographis paniculata (King of Bitters)

Dubbed the "King of Bitters," every part of the plant features an extraordinarily bitter profile due to andrographolide compounds.

Asystasia gangetica (Chinese Violet)

A vigorous, spreading tropical perennial herb native to Afro-Eurasia. It bears attractive, creamy-white or light violet bell-shaped flowers aligned on one side of the stem.

Odontonema tubaeforme (Firespike)

It features large, glossy, wavy-edged leaves and produces upright, dramatic spikes packed with bright crimson, tubular flowers that bloom reliably from late summer through winter, acting as a magnet for pollinators.
